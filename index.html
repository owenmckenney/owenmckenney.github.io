Research Experience and Interests

I currently conduct research under the direction of Dr. Hilary Bart-Smith and Dr. Joseph Zhu and have been doing so since Fall 2023. My work here has mainly focused on developing control strategies for bio-inspired swimming platforms, including a significant amount of work with the lab's Tunabot and Mantabot robotic vehicles. I also had the opportunity to conduct research under the direction of Dr. Sarah Sun during Spring 2025 where I built a simulation environment for motion planning for use in EEG-controlled robotic manipulation.

I am actively searching for PhD programs for Fall 2026! I am specifically interested in labs working on biomimetic approaches in robotics for applications in locomotion and manipulation.

My motivation: Biological systems embody millions of years of optimization, and I see immense potential in drawing from these principles to advance robotics in both fundamental and applied domains. I aim to advance robotic efficiency and adaptability through the co-design of bio-inspired morphology and control, treating physical design and computation as a unified system to achieve intelligent behavior. I seek to accomplish this by pairing rigorous theoretical modeling that captures key robot behaviors with computation driven sensing and control that allow robots to leverage their morphology for efficient, adaptive behavior.

Publications

Development of a turning control strategy for a bio-inspired underwater vehicle.
Owen McKenney, Joseph Zhu, Tianjun Han, Hilary Bart-Smith
Bioinspiration & Biomimetics, 2025, 10.1088/1748-3190/adf67a

I developed a single-actuator turning and control strategy for a tuna-inspired swimming vehicle, Tunabot. Frequency asymmetry between the left and right half-strokes of the full path of the tailbeat cycle was introduced, leading to a turn. Scaling laws for tangential velocity $V$, turning radius $r$, and turning rate $\omega$ were developed to model the behavior of the vehicle based on the base flapping frequency $f$ and asymmetric frequency difference $\Delta f$.

$$ r = c_{2}\,\phi_{2}, \qquad \phi_{2} = \frac{f\,(f-\Delta f) + (f-\Delta f)^{2}}{f\,(f-\Delta f) - (f-\Delta f)^{2}} $$

Selected Research Projects

Virtual Environment for UR5 Manipulator
I created a simulation environment for a Universal Robotics' UR5 using Python coupled with ROS2 and MoveIt for control and motion planning. A camera was used to capture the actual workspace and mirror objects into the simulation, enabling accurate pick-and-place. Software will be used to carry out electroencephalogram (EEG) robotic manipulation testing with human participants. Work in progress.

Autonomous navigation for a manta-ray inspired robot (Mantabot)
I implemented a vison and IMU based path planning strategy for a manta-inspired swimming vehicle, Mantabot. Paths to ArUco tags were generated using onboard cameras and tracked using pure-pursuit coupled with PID control. I also redesigned the circuitry and control PCB for the vehicle (see Technical Work section). Work in progress.

Bio-inspired riverine hydrokinetic energy generatator demonstration
I designed and fabricated a demonstration system for a riverine hydrokinetic energy generator that exploited unsteady lift-based hydrodynamics of pitching hydrofoils to harvest energy. I developed a scaled model, designed components and printed circuit boards, and wrote C++ code to mimic sinusoidal hydrofoil behavior. My demonstration system was presented at the ARPA-E Innovation Summit 2024.

Double Pendulum Simulation
I derived the equations of motion for a double pendulum and implemented them in Python, using a 4th-order Runge-Kutta integrator to simulate the system's angular dynamics over time.

Vision-Based UR5 Human-Robot Interaction
I used a webcam and a Universal Robots' UR5 manipulator to allow for the end effector to deliver a cube to a dynamically moving human hand. I used PID controllers to guide the end effector to the human hand, scaling speed as target became closer.

3D Printed Planetary Robotic Actuator
I designed a custom actuator for bipedal and quadrupedal robotics. The actuator used a planetary gearbox to achieve 8:1 reduction and is fully backdriveable. The design uses mjbots motors, drivers, and magnetic encoders for control.
