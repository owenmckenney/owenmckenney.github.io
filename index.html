Research Experience and Interests

I currently conduct research under the direction of Dr. Hilary Bart-Smith and Dr. Joseph Zhu and have been doing so since Fall 2023. My work here has mainly focused on developing control strategies for bio-inspired swimming platforms, including a significant amount of work with the lab's Tunabot and Mantabot robotic vehicles. I also had the opportunity to conduct research under the direction of Dr. Sarah Sun during Spring 2025 where I built a simulation environment for motion planning for use in EEG-controlled robotic manipulation.

I am actively searching for PhD programs and labs for the Fall/Winter 2025 application cycle! Specifically, I am interested in programs that would enable me to continue my work in the bio-inspired and novel robotics field.

I am very interested in the design and control of these bio-inspired systems and believe that manueverable, efficient, low-cost, novel robots will eventually become commonplace for navigating adverse environments and completing tasks not suited for traditional robotics. I also think that the traditional robotics space has a lot to learn from bio-inspired design, particulary when it comes to efficient locomotion and grasping. I hope to be able to make contributions in this space through intelligent mechanical design and software control.
