Research Experience and Interests

I currently conduct research under the direction of Dr. Hilary Bart-Smith and Dr. Joseph Zhu and have been doing so since Fall 2023. My work here has mainly focused on developing control strategies for bio-inspired swimming platforms, including a significant amount of work with the lab's Tunabot and Mantabot robotic vehicles. I also had the opportunity to conduct research under the direction of Dr. Sarah Sun during Spring 2025 where I built a simulation environment for motion planning for use in EEG-controlled robotic manipulation.

I am actively searching for PhD programs for Fall 2026! Broadly, I am interested in bioinspired and novel design, biomimetic sensing, and machine learning for applications in robotic locomotion and manipulation. I also have interests in fluid dynamics and control.

My motivation: Biological systems embody millions of years of optimization, and I see immense potential in drawing from these principles to advance robotics in both fundamental and applied domains. I aim to advance robotic efficiency and adaptability through the co-design of bio-inspired morphology and control, treating physical design and computation as a unified system to achieve intelligent behavior. I seek to accomplish this by pairing rigorous theoretical modeling that captures key robot behaviors with computation driven sensing and control that allow robots to leverage their morphology for efficient, adaptive behavior.

Publications

Development of a turning control strategy for a bio-inspired underwater vehicle.
Owen McKenney, Joseph Zhu, Tianjun Han, Hilary Bart-Smith
Bioinspiration & Biomimetics, 2025, 10.1088/1748-3190/adf67a

I developed a single-actuator turning and control strategy for a tuna-inspired swimming vehicle, Tunabot. Frequency asymmetry between the left and right half-strokes of the full path of the tailbeat cycle was introduced, leading to a turn. Scaling laws for tangential velocity $V$, turning radius $r$, and turning rate $\omega$ were developed to model the behavior of the vehicle based on the base flapping frequency $f_1f$ and asymmetric frequency difference $\Delta f$.

$$ r = c_{2}\,\phi_{2}, \qquad \phi_{2} = \frac{f_1^2\,(f_1-\Delta f) + (f_1-\Delta f)^2\,f_1}{f_1^2\,(f_1-\Delta f) - (f_1-\Delta f)^2\,f_1} $$

Selected Research Projects

Virtual Environment for UR5 Manipulator
I created a simulation environment for a Universal Robotics' UR5 using Python coupled with ROS2 and MoveIt for control and motion planning. A camera was used to capture the actual workspace and mirror objects into the simulation, enabling accurate pick-and-place. Objects in this demo (blue cubes) represent actual cubes in the workspace and change dynamically as real objects are moved. Software will be used to carry out electroencephalogram (EEG) robotic manipulation testing with human participants. Work in progress.

Autonomous navigation for a manta-ray inspired robot (Mantabot)
I implemented a vison and IMU based path planning strategy for a manta-inspired swimming vehicle, Mantabot. Paths to ArUco tags were generated using onboard cameras and tracked using pure-pursuit coupled with PID control. I also redesigned the circuitry and control PCB for the vehicle (see Technical Work section). Work in progress.

Bio-inspired riverine hydrokinetic energy generatator demonstration
I designed and fabricated a demonstration system for a riverine hydrokinetic energy generator that exploits the unsteady lift-based hydrodynamics of pitching hydrofoils to harvest energy. I built a scaled system, designing custom SolidWorks components, a control circuit board in Altium, and wrote embedded C++ to drive the actuators. Using closed-loop control, I synchronized linear actuators and servos to produce sinusoidal hydrofoil motion with tunable frequency and amplitude. I assisted in the presentation of this system at the ARPA-E Innovation Summit 2024.

3D Printed Planetary Robotic Actuator
Github Repository: https://github.com/owenmckenney/Robotic_Actuator
I designed a custom actuator for bipedal and quadrupedal robotics. The actuator uses a planetary gearbox to achieve an 8:1 reduction and is fully backdriveable. The design uses mjbots motors, drivers, and magnetic encoders for control, and I provide a BOM and 3D-printable STL files for fabrication and assembly. I control the actuator using a Raspberry Pi with an mjbots pi3hat (via the Python scripts in my GitHub repositiory).
